Aguachile

Description

Aguachile is a zesty Mexican seafood dish featuring raw shrimp marinated in a vibrant, spicy sauce made from lime juice, fresh chilies, cilantro, and sometimes garlic. The lime juice gently "cooks" the shrimp, giving it a tender texture, while the sauce delivers a bold, tangy, and fiery kick. Often garnished with sliced red onion or cucumber for crunch, aguachile is typically served chilled with tostadas or tortilla chips, making it a refreshing coastal delicacy, especially popular in Sinaloa, Mexico.

Ingredients

  • Fresh shrimp
  • Lime juice
  • Serrano or jalape√±o peppers
  • Cilantro
  • Red onion
  • Cucumber
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Peel and slice shrimp thinly.
  2. Blend lime juice, chilies, cilantro, and salt into a sauce.
  3. Pour sauce over shrimp and marinate for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Slice red onion and cucumber, then mix in.
  5. Serve chilled with tostadas or tortilla chips.
  6. Enjoy!
